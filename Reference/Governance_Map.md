## COBIT 2019 Core Model

### EDM — Evaluate, Direct and Monitor (Governance)
* **EDM01** — Ensured Governance Framework Setting and Maintenance: Establish and maintain the governance framework.
* **EDM02** — Ensured Benefits Delivery: Make sure I&T-enabled investments deliver intended value.
* **EDM03** — Ensured Risk Optimization: Direct and oversee I&T risk within appetite.
* **EDM04** — Ensured Resource Optimization: Ensure efficient use of I&T resources.
* **EDM05** — Ensured Stakeholder Engagement: Ensure transparency and trust with stakeholders.

### APO — Align, Plan and Organize (Management)
* **APO01** — Managed I&T Management Framework: Design and run the management system.
* **APO02** — Managed Strategy: Define I&T strategy aligned to enterprise goals.
* **APO03** — Managed Enterprise Architecture: Maintain architecture for strategy and interoperability.
* **APO04** — Managed Innovation: Identify and adopt value-creating innovations.
* **APO05** — Managed Portfolio: Govern the investment portfolio (risk/value/performance).
* **APO06** — Managed Budget and Costs: Plan and optimize I&T budgeting.
* **APO07** — Managed Human Resources: Ensure staffing, skills, and performance.
* **APO08** — Managed Relationships: Manage business–IT alignment and satisfaction.
* **APO09** — Managed Service Agreements: Define and monitor SLAs/OLAs.
* **APO10** — Managed Vendors: Manage third-party contracts, risk, and compliance.
* **APO11** — Managed Quality: Establish quality management for services/solutions.
* **APO12** — Managed Risk: Identify and treat I&T-related risk.
* **APO13** — Managed Security: Define and operate the information security program.
* **APO14** — Managed Data: Govern data quality, lifecycle, and protection.

### BAI — Build, Acquire and Implement (Management)
* **BAI01** — Managed Programs: Manage programs to deliver coordinated benefits.
* **BAI02** — Managed Requirements Definition: Define and validate business requirements.
* **BAI03** — Managed Solutions Identification and Build: Design, build, and test solutions.
* **BAI04** — Managed Availability and Capacity: Plan performance/capacity for demand.
* **BAI05** — Managed Organizational Change: Support adoption and training for change.
* **BAI06** — Managed IT Changes: Control changes to reduce disruption (Change Management).
* **BAI07** — Managed IT Change Acceptance: Transition solutions into operations.
* **BAI08** — Managed Knowledge: Maintain documentation and known errors.
* **BAI09** — Managed Assets: Lifecycle management of I&T assets.
* **BAI10** — Managed Configuration: Maintain CMDB and configuration records.
* **BAI11** — Managed Projects: Manage project scope, schedule, cost, and quality.

### DSS — Deliver, Service and Support (Management)
* **DSS01** — Managed Operations: Run day-to-day operations (backups, monitoring).
* **DSS02** — Managed Service Requests and Incidents: Handle user requests and incidents.
* **DSS03** — Managed Problems: Root cause analysis and known error management.
* **DSS04** — Managed Continuity: BCP/DR planning and testing.
* **DSS05** — Managed Security Services: Operational security (IAM, vuln management).
* **DSS06** — Managed Business Process Controls: Automated application controls.

### MEA — Monitor, Evaluate and Assess (Management)
* **MEA01** — Managed Performance Monitoring: Monitor conformance to targets.
* **MEA02** — Managed System of Internal Control: Monitor internal control effectiveness.
* **MEA03** — Managed Compliance: Manage external legal/regulatory obligations.
* **MEA04** — Managed Assurance: Plan and track audit/assurance activities.

## ITIL v4 Practices

### General Management Practices
* **Architecture Management:** Keep architectures coherent so products/services evolve predictably.
* **Continual Improvement:** Run improvement as a disciplined practice (identify, prioritize, measure, iterate).
* **Information Security Management:** Protect confidentiality, integrity, availability via policy, controls, and assurance.
* **Knowledge Management:** Capture and share useful knowledge so work is faster and repeatable.
* **Measurement and Reporting:** Define what to measure, collect it, and report it to drive decisions.
* **Organizational Change Management:** Prepare people/roles/behaviors so changes get adopted.
* **Portfolio Management:** Govern the mix of services/products/projects to optimize value and risk.
* **Project Management:** Deliver initiatives with control of scope, time, cost, quality, and risk.
* **Relationship Management:** Maintain effective relationships with customers and stakeholders.
* **Risk Management:** Identify/analyze/treat risk and keep it within appetite.
* **Service Financial Management:** Budgeting/accounting/charging for service costs.
* **Strategy Management:** Define direction and ensure service management supports business goals.
* **Supplier Management:** Select/manage suppliers and contracts to meet performance/compliance.
* **Workforce and Talent Management:** Ensure the right skills, staffing, development, and performance.

### Service Management Practices
* **Availability Management:** Ensure services are available as agreed when users need them.
* **Business Analysis:** Translate business needs into clear requirements and outcomes.
* **Capacity and Performance Management:** Make sure performance meets demand now and in the future.
* **Change Enablement:** Assess/authorize/coordinate changes to reduce risk and disruption.
* **Incident Management:** Restore service quickly and minimize business impact.
* **IT Asset Management:** Control assets through lifecycle (inventory, ownership, disposal).
* **Monitoring and Event Management:** Detect service/component states and trigger action.
* **Problem Management:** Find root causes, manage known errors, prevent recurrence.
* **Release Management:** Plan and control releases into environments.
* **Service Catalog Management:** Maintain a reliable source of truth for services and offerings.
* **Service Configuration Management:** Maintain configuration info (CMDB) to support decisions.
* **Service Continuity Management:** Prepare for and recover from major disruptions (DR).
* **Service Design:** Design services to meet requirements across the lifecycle.
* **Service Desk:** Provide a user-facing entry point for requests/incidents.
* **Service Level Management:** Define, agree, monitor, and improve service levels (SLAs).
* **Service Request Management:** Fulfill standard user requests efficiently.
* **Service Validation and Testing:** Ensure new/changed services meet requirements (QA).

### Technical Management Practices
* **Deployment Management:** Move new/changed components into target environments.
* **Infrastructure and Platform Management:** Operate and evolve infrastructure/platforms.
* **Software Development and Management:** Build/acquire, test, release, and maintain software.
